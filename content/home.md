Hi, I am Zhejia Dong, a Ph.D. candidate in the [Department of Biostatistics](https://biostatistics.sph.brown.edu/) at [Brown University](https://www.brown.edu/).

My research focuses on developing statistical and causal inference methods for observational and randomized studies, with applications in public health and the social sciences. Broadly, I am interested in methodological challenges arising from complex data structures, including network dependence, interference, missing data, and real-world health data. My recent work includes matching and randomization inference for observational network data, as well as methods for disentangling network dependence across multiple variables. I am also interested in integrating machine learning tools with principled causal inference methods for electronic health record data.
